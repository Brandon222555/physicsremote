About PhysicsRemote

Curated under-the-radar remote jobs for physicists who code.

Why this site exists

Physics grads are one of the most underused talent pools in tech. The skills you build in a lab — Monte Carlo, Bayesian inference, numerical methods, PDE/ODE solvers, signal processing — transfer directly into roles paying $130k–$300k base. But the job market doesn’t know how to surface those matches.

PhysicsRemote is a small, opinionated list of real openings where a physics+ML background is an advantage, compensation is verifiable, and remote actually means remote.

Who it’s for

  • Physics BS/MS/PhD with 1–10 years of experience
  • Comfortable in Python, familiar with modern ML tooling
  • Targeting $100k+ base, fully remote (US or globally distributed)
  • Willing to apply directly through company sites

How roles are selected

A role is added only if it clears five checks: Remote-friendly, pays $100k+ base (verifiable), under-the-radar (not the obvious 20 places), physics-fit (rated 1–5 stars), and legit (careers page loads, company has real funding).

How often it updates

Every 2 weeks a scheduled task searches for new openings, verifies each candidate, adds 8–15 fresh roles, and retires stale ones. The last update date is shown at the top of the jobs page.

FAQ

Are these salaries guaranteed?

No. Ranges are base salary targets for mid-level US roles from public sources. Bonus + equity often add 30–100% more. Always verify on Levels.fyi.

Why no Google, Meta, Citadel, Jane Street?

They already have your attention. PhysicsRemote surfaces places you didn’t already know to apply.

I’m not in the US — can I still use this?

Several listings are tagged remote-global or remote-EU/US. Filter by those tags.

How do I actually get one of these jobs?

Apply directly through company careers pages, lead with transferable quantitative work, and prep for domain-specific interviews (order-statistics for quant, physics-informed nets for scientific ML, mechanistic interp for safety).
